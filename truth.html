Truth

Entry ID: SRC-NODE-00001
Core Concept Knowledge High Confidence

Truth is the quality of being in accordance with fact, reality, evidence, or verified understanding.

“Truth is not just what is said. In SourceRoot, truth is what can be traced, sourced, revised, and understood through its relationships.”

Origin

Language Origin

trēowþ

Old English: faithfulness, loyalty, firmness, or trustworthiness.

Conceptual Origin

The modern concept of truth connects factual accuracy, trust, reality, verification, and knowledge.

Meaning Timeline

Old English
Faithfulness and loyalty

Truth was closely tied to trust, promise-keeping, fidelity, and reliability.

Middle English
Honesty and sincerity

The meaning expanded toward truthful speech and personal integrity.

Modern English
Conformity with reality or fact

Truth became closely connected to evidence, verification, logic, and objective reality.

Related Concepts

Evidence Truth is supported by evidence.
Verification Truth is confirmed through verification.
Knowledge Truth is foundational to knowledge.
Trust Truth and trust share historical and conceptual roots.
Reality Truth corresponds to what is real or actual.
Belief Belief may align with, diverge from, or seek truth.
