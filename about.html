The Mission

Make knowledge traceable.

Most digital knowledge is presented as a final answer. SourceRoot takes a different approach. It asks where the answer came from, what supports it, how it changed, who contributed to it, and what other ideas it connects to.

DictionaryHub starts with familiar concepts like Truth, Knowledge, Evidence, Trust, and Verification. But the larger vision is a universal knowledge graph where entries carry their origin, relationships, sources, and revision history.

Core Principles

What SourceRoot stands for.

1

Provenance First

Knowledge should show where it came from, what supports it, and how it has changed over time.

2

Everything Connects

Concepts do not exist alone. They gain meaning through relationships with other concepts, sources, and histories.

3

History Matters

A revision history turns knowledge into a living record instead of a static page.

4

Sources Matter

Claims become stronger when they are tied to transparent references and supporting evidence.

5

Human + AI Ready

AI can suggest connections, but trust requires sources, validation, and revision trails.

6

Built to Last

The long-term goal is knowledge infrastructure that can outlive platforms, formats, and individual contributors.

The Protocol Model

How a SourceRoot entry works.

A SourceRoot entry is more than a definition. Each entry can include identity, meaning, relationships, sources, and revision history.

Node The concept, word, person, source, event, or idea.
Origin Where the entry came from and how it began.
Relationships How the entry connects to other knowledge.
Sources References that support or explain the entry.
Revisions A history of what changed and why.

Roadmap

Where this goes next.

Phase 1

Static Prototype

Homepage, Explore, Concept, Graph, Sources, Revisions, and About pages powered by a small JSON seed graph.

Phase 2

Expanded Data

Grow nodes.json from a handful of concepts to a larger curated seed graph with stronger sources and relationship types.

Phase 3

Structured Graph Database

Move from JSON files into a real graph database when the dataset becomes too large for simple static files.

Phase 4

Contributor Layer

Add identity, suggestions, validation, approval workflows, reputation, and revision comparison.

Phase 5

Protocol Ecosystem

Support APIs, partner applications, AI provenance, public archives, and long-term preservation systems.
